Bachelor’s Programs in Germany

Germany offers a wide range of bachelor’s programs taught in both German and English. Many universities are state funded, meaning there are little to no tuition fees for international students. Programs span engineering, business, social sciences and more.

Popular Universities

  • Technical University of Munich (TUM)
  • RWTH Aachen University
  • University of Stuttgart
  • University of Mannheim
  • Humboldt University of Berlin

Admission Basics

Admission generally requires a higher secondary certificate equivalent to the German Abitur, proof of language proficiency (German or English, depending on the course), and a motivational statement. Certain programs may request entrance examinations or portfolios.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know German? Many programs are available in English, but basic German helps with daily life. Check each university’s language policy.

What about tuition fees? Most public universities charge only a semester contribution, typically between €200–€500 per term.

How do I apply? Applications usually go through uni-assist or the university’s own portal. Deadlines are typically mid-July for the winter semester.
